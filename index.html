About HuskyHacks v3.0

Hackathon: an event in which computer programmers and others involved in software development and hardware development, including graphic designers, interface designers and project managers, collaborate intensively.



HuskyHacks 3.0 is the third iteration of Northeastern University's 24-hour hackathon. On March 25th, over 200 undergraduate students will come together in West Village H to form teams of up to 4 to build something amazing. The possibilities are limitless: learn a new language, power on your Particle for the first time, try on an Oculus Rift, and turn your dreams into reality. If this will be your first hackathon, have no fear; Mentors with a wide variety of skill sets will be present to assist competitors with their projects. Students will be allowed to innovate using any software or hardware tools, as long as they are working on a fresh project. Participants will be accepted on a first come, first serve basis.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is it?

HuskyHacks will take place March 25-26 from 10:00am to 3:00pm.

How much does it cost?

HuskyHacks is completely free and includes free food, drinks, and swag.

What should I bring?

All you need is your student ID, a laptop+charger, and any hardware you want to hack on.

Who can come?

HuskyHacks this semester is open to any undergraduates at any international institutions.

Do I have to know how to code?

Absolutely not! A hackathon is the perfect place and time to learn. There will be mentors available to ease you through your first hackathon experience.

How do teams work?

Teams can be up to 4 people. All teamates must register individually.

Will there be hardware?

We are working to secure hardware, and participants are welcome to bring their own hardware to hack on.

What can I win?

Money and Arduinos and stuff. But is that really why you go to hackathons?
